        <section class="info-card">
            <h2>So What Is Global Warming?</h2>
            <div class="alternating-row">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <p>Merriam-Webster defines <span class="highlight">global warming</span> as "an increase in the earth's atmospheric and oceanic temperatures widely predicted to occur due to an increase in the greenhouse effect resulting especially from pollution."</p>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/globalwarming1.jpg" alt="Global Warming Definition" class="small-img">
            </div>
            <div class="alternating-row reverse">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <p>Human activities like driving, farming, and manufacturing release greenhouse gases (CO<sub>2</sub>, methane, and nitrous oxide) that thicken the atmosphere. This layer acts like a <strong>"heat trap,"</strong> allowing sunlight to enter but preventing heat from escaping back into space. As more heat is reflected back toward the surface rather than being released, the global temperature rises, leading to climate change.</p>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/globalwarming2.jpg" alt="Greenhouse Effect Mechanism" class="mechanism-img">
            </div>
        </section>

        <!-- SECTION 2: WHY IT MATTERS (8 IMPACT CARDS) -->
        <section class="info-card">
            <h2>Why Does Global Warming Matter?</h2>
            <div class="impact-grid">
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Health and Clean Air:</strong> Increasing carbon emissions compromise air quality, which directly impacts human health. This leads to a rise in asthma, heart disease, and lung conditions, especially in children.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Water Security:</strong> Even small temperature increases destabilize the water cycle, leading to severe water scarcity for some and frequent life-treating flooding for others.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Destruction of "The World's Lungs":</strong> Precious ecosystems like the Amazon rainforest and coral reefs are reaching breaking points. When these systems fail, they stop absorbing CO<sub>2</sub> and start contributing to the problem.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Threatens Food and Drink:</strong> Basic necessities are at risk. In 2020 almost 150 million people were exposed to severe food insecurity, a number which is expected to exceed 1 billion by 2100.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Responsibility to the Future:</strong> We currently enjoy a diverse and nurturing planet; future generations deserve to inherit that same world rather than one defined by "sweaty Decembers" and resource scarcity.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>It Hits Your Wallet:</strong> Climate change isn't just environmental; it's economic. Droughts and floods make groceries more expensive, and extreme weather leads to higher costs.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>Loss of Iconic Wildlife:</strong> Many species, including polar bears, snow leopards, and sea turtles, are losing their habitats and food sources faster than they can adapt.
                </div>
                <div class="impact-item">
                    <strong>It Affects Everyone:</strong> Climate change is an issue for everyone; it disrupts global supply chains, affects local businesses, and even impacts pets (such as sidewalks becoming too hot for paws).
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>

        <!-- SECTION 3: THE WASTE CONNECTION -->
        <section class="info-card">
            <h2>Why should I care, I'm not running pollution factories?</h2>
            <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/whyIcare.jpg" alt="Why I Care" class="why-care-img">
            <p>The way you dispose waste has a major impact on climate change:</p>
            <ul class="detail-list">
                <li><strong>Methane Powerhouse:</strong> The waste sector accounts for approximately <strong>20%</strong> of global human-driven methane emissions. In the short term, methane is over <strong>80 times</strong> more potent than CO<sub>2</sub> at trapping heat.</li>
                <li><strong>The "Oxygen-Free" Problem:</strong> When organic waste—such as food, paper, cardboard, and wood—is buried in landfills, it decomposes in an <strong>anaerobic (oxygen-free)</strong> environment. This creates "landfill gas," a potent mixture of methane and CO<sub>2</sub>.</li>
                <li><strong>Slow Decay, Long-Term Impact:</strong> Unlike pollutants that dissipate quickly, organic matter in landfills decays slowly over decades. Discarded waste today will emit greenhouse gases for <strong>generations</strong>.</li>
                <li><strong>Food Waste is the Primary Culprit:</strong> Globally, organic waste comprises about <strong>65%</strong> of all municipal trash. Highly reactive food and green waste are the primary source of methane within the waste stream.</li>
                <li><strong>A Growing Crisis:</strong> Global waste is projected to increase by <strong>73%</strong> by 2050. Without improved systems, methane emissions from waste are expected to surge by <strong>13 megatons</strong> per year over the next decade alone.</li>
            </ul>
        </section>

<!-- SECTION 4: GLOBAL & LOCAL ACTION -->
        <section class="info-card">
            <h2>So what is the world doing to prevent global warming?</h2>
            
            <div class="alternating-row">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <h3>Global Action: Paris Agreement</h3>
                    <p>The consensus is that while the Paris Agreement has successfully lowered the "worst-case" warming projections (moving us away from a 4°C+ future), current pledges still put the world on a path closer to <strong>2.5°C–2.8°C</strong> unless implementation accelerates significantly.</p>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/ParisAgreement.jpg" alt="Paris Agreement" class="small-img">
            </div>

            <p>Since 2022, the U.S. has accelerated its climate strategy through several key actions:</p>
            <ul class="detail-list">
                <li><strong>Clean Energy Funding:</strong> The federal government is deploying <strong>hundreds of billions</strong> in tax credits from the <a href="https://www.energy.gov/edf/inflation-reduction-act-2022" target="_blank">Inflation Reduction Act</a> to triple wind and solar capacity and subsidize domestic battery manufacturing.</li>
                <li><strong>Emissions Regulation:</strong> The EPA has implemented <a href="https://eelp.law.harvard.edu/tracker/epa-voc-and-methane-standards-for-oil-and-gas-facilities-2/" target="_blank">new mandates</a> to significantly reduce methane leaks from oil wells and increase the average fuel efficiency of new passenger vehicles to <strong>49 mpg by 2026</strong>.</li>
                <li><strong>Infrastructure Investment:</strong> National agencies are <a href="https://www.transportation.gov/rural/ev/toolkit/ev-infrastructure-funding-and-financing/federal-funding-programs" target="_blank">funding the installation</a> of <strong>500,000</strong> electric vehicle charging stations and upgrading the power grid to support the transition to a low-carbon economy.</li>
            </ul>

            <!-- CAMBRIDGE ACTION SUBSECTION -->
            <div class="icon-text-block" style="overflow: auto;">
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/Cambridgenetzero.jpg" alt="Cambridge Net Zero Plan" style="float: right; width: 150px; margin-left: 20px; border-radius: 8px;">
                
                <h3>Cambridge Action</h3>
                <p>Cambridge’s action is driven by its <a href="https://www.cambridgema.gov/Departments/officeofsustainability/News/2025/09/cambridgerollsoutsupportforbuildingownersasemissionrequirementsbegin" target="_blank">Building Energy Use Disclosure Ordinance (BEUDO)</a>.</p>
                
                <ul class="detail-list">
                    <li><strong>The 2035 Cliff:</strong> While most cities aim for 2050 for total decarbonization, Cambridge amended BEUDO to require all non-residential buildings over <strong>100,000 square feet</strong> (think the big biotech labs in Kendall Square) to reach <strong>Net Zero by 2035</strong>.</li>
                    <li><strong>The Economic Lever:</strong> If a building doesn't meet the target, they must pay a "compliance fee" into a green fund. This makes Cambridge the first city in the U.S. to effectively put a hard, local <strong>"expiration date"</strong> on fossil fuel use for large commercial properties.</li>
                </ul>
            </div>

            <h3>Why Cambridge is unique:</h3>
            <p><strong>Kendall Square.</strong> Biotech labs require massive amounts of energy for ventilation and <strong>24/7 cooling</strong>. In 2026, Cambridge is the global <strong>"test case"</strong> for whether a high-tech economy can actually run on zero emissions.</p>

    <!-- SECTION 6: THE HARM OF INTUITION -->
        <section class="info-card">
            <h2>What's the harm in just using my intuition?</h2>
            <p>Improperly sorted recycle does at least one of the four things:</p>

            <!-- 1. Turns Recyclables into Garbage -->
            <div class="alternating-row">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <h3>It Turns Recyclables into Garbage:</h3>
                    <p>The most significant impact of contamination is that it often ruins perfectly good recyclables.</p>
                    <ul class="detail-list">
                        <li><strong>The "Spoiling" Effect:</strong> Items like greasy pizza boxes or unrinsed yogurt containers can leak onto paper and cardboard, making them impossible to process.</li>
                        <li><strong>Entire Batches Lost:</strong> If a load is too contaminated, recycling facilities cannot economically sort it. Consequently, as much as <strong>20% of processed material</strong> (including the clean items) is diverted to landfills or incineration.</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/improperrecycles.jpg" alt="Improperly Sorted Items" class="mechanism-img">
            </div>

            <!-- 2. Damages Sorting Machinery -->
            <div class="alternating-row reverse">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <h3>It Damages Sorting Machinery</h3>
                    <p>Recycling centers rely on complex systems of conveyor belts and sensors that are not designed for "wishcycled" items.</p>
                    <ul class="detail-list">
                        <li><strong>Clogs and Jams:</strong> Plastic bags, hoses, and <strong>"tanglers"</strong> can wrap around rotating sorting drums, forcing the entire facility to shut down for manual repairs.</li>
                        <li><strong>Sensor Interference:</strong> Materials like <strong>black plastics</strong> often cannot be detected by optical sorters, causing them to end up in the wrong streams and further contaminating the end products.</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/tangler.jpg" alt="Black Plastic Interference" class="mechanism-img">
            </div>

            <!-- 3. Threatens Worker Safety -->
            <div class="alternating-row">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <h3>It Threatens Worker Safety</h3>
                    <p>Humans are at the front lines of the sorting process, and contamination puts them at direct physical risk.</p>
                    <ul class="detail-list">
                        <li><strong>Hazardous Materials:</strong> Batteries, <strong>"sharps" (needles)</strong>, and chemical containers can cause <strong>fires, explosions, or puncture wounds</strong> when they reach the sorting line.</li>
                        <li><strong>Increased Handling:</strong> When bins are messy, workers must spend more time manually handling trash, increasing their exposure to these hazards.</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/Workersinsorter.jpg" alt="Sorting Facility Workers" class="mechanism-img">
            </div>

            <!-- 4. Increases Costs for Everyone -->
            <div class="alternating-row reverse">
                <div class="alternating-text">
                    <h3>It Increases Costs for Everyone</h3>
                    <p>Contamination makes the business of recycling less economically viable.</p>
                    <ul class="detail-list">
                        <li><strong>Higher Processing Fees:</strong> Cleaning and re-sorting contaminated batches can increase recycling costs by <strong>up to 25%</strong>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Taxpayer Impact:</strong> These additional operational costs are eventually passed back to consumers and taxpayers through <strong>increased waste management fees</strong>.</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                <img src="Ourcycle_whysort_images/highcost.jpg" alt="Economic Impact of Contamination" class="mechanism-img">
            </div>
