# OurCycle
**OurCycle: Cambridge Waste & Upcycling Masterlist**
OurCycle is an educational web platform designed to help Cambridge, MA residents transition from "unconscious daily habits" to sustainable, creative waste routines. Developed as part of the MIT Leadership Training Institute (LTI), the site centralizes local recycling data, composting guides, and community upcycling resources into a single, user-friendly interface.

**🌍 Project Mission****
To provide the inspiration and knowledge needed to turn waste currently destined for landfills into valuable recoverable resources. By addressing the confusion surrounding "wishcycling," OurCycle aims to reduce contamination in local waste streams and mitigate environmental impacts like methane emissions.

**🚀 Key Features**
Interactive Disposal Guide: A 24-category grid on the homepage for instant access to specific disposal rules (e.g., textiles, hazardous waste, electronics).  

**The Masterlist:** A technical deep-dive with anchor-link navigation for precise waste management instructions.  

**Impact Dashboard:** Real-time visualization of Cambridge-specific waste statistics to build community awareness. 

Importance of Proper Waste Disposal at Individual level: Understanding of how our waste streams can contribute to global warming. Statistics and combating strategies at global and city levels are also detailed.

**Journey of Our Waste:** A summarized version on where different waste streams from Cambridge end up. How our sorting helps the planet.

**Community Resource Directory:** A curated list of local thrift stores, pickup organizations, and social media groups to encourage a circular economy.

**Upcycling Projects:** A curated list of upcycling ideas with links to external sources, driven by the fact that reuse is better than recycle.
 

**🛠️ Technical Stack**
HTML5/CSS3: Utilizes a responsive, mobile-friendly grid system and a "Sticky" navigation architecture for high usability.

**Custom UI:** Features a specialized color palette (e.g., Primary Green, Warm Beige) designed for readability and professional aesthetics.

**Modular Code:** Built with a clean separation of navigation and content, allowing for easy expansion as local programs evolve.
